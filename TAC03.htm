Also known as "Aught Three with TAC"

The winds of change carry with them indications that you have utterly failed to keep true to your rural roots.  Thrice previously you have been urged to hasten from the cities where you dwell and lead, as does the mass of men, lives of quiet desperation.  I challenge thee to consider the naked lady adorned shores of Pace Bend Park, the green rolling hills of Muleshoe Bend, or the dramatic scenery of Perdenales Falls instead of the deluxe accommodations of Clark Castle with its flush toilets, hot and cold running faucets, patio, stereo, hoop and sleeping berths.  I know the temptation to stay within ten miles of bresticulating beer hostesses, and glaze-laden, fat-fried yeast cakes is almost insurmountable.  But consider the convenience of arising from your seat and lifting its lid to retrieve an ale, rather than making the pilgrimage from the patio to the fridge enduring an extreme range in temperature. Or ponder the necessity of lifting and lowering the lid instead of the immediate gratification of  zip and whiz.  As a concession, the ancestors are willing to acknowledge a move in this direction without requiring a piscatorial effort.  Consider, gentlemen, the cicada's song to what your host will undoubtedly play incessantly all weekend, Gabriel or worse, Sumner.

In any event, you are familiar with the prerequisites: the memorial hack, poker (aka give Rewd money), tip or a tip variant, childish role-playing, darts if available, and hoochenany.  I propose, tentatively and as a patriotic nod, Flag Day.  No, Flag Day, Ferret.  June 14th.  After all, summer starts on the 21st and the Fahrenheit could approach 82 degrees.  Oh, bust a nut, that Sunday is Father's Day, and it occurs to me that a few of you have sired pups of one hideous nature or another.  So be it; the Summer Solstice it is then  June 20-22, aught 3.  So avail me of your availability, so I know when to call the Texas Parks and Wildlife Department.  Stay at Rodman's abode if you must, but be rest assured that all transmissions from this side to your once great organization shall cease.

I heard that.

Ubiquitously momentarily yours,

Zoroaster
