Augdreams

I climb a stairway to heaven,

Which leads to a Keep on the Borderlands,

Where it happens all the time --

People just explode

Natural causes.

I'm drawn to a tent in the Market Square.

I find a man inside, whose dick, I must confess,

Is bigger than mine.

He asks my name.

I mumble, "Prickskin," still stunned.

"Pliskin," he says, "I heard of you;

Heard you were dead."

A small but muscular woman enters.

Unphased by the exhibition,

She draws me out and says, "I'm Tina."

I glance back toward the tent.

The man is gone,

But a pile of steaming spaghetti has appeared.

Her skin glistening in the sun,

Tina escorts me to a saloon.

As we enter, a man falls from the rafters.

It appears that he is hurt.

A band is playing,

And the best thing about them?

None of them is Sting.

Tina buys me a Rattlesnake Cooler

And a Dragon's Breath

And a Jack & Coke

And a bong hit

And a Lark's Vomit

And a shroom ... I smile.

"Valkyrie," I say lustily.

"Uh, it's Tina."

"You know what would be cool right now?

Not with a crossbow, but

The friendly barkeep sends me home,

But hands me a coil of wire.

"12 gauge," he says proudly,

Full moon tonight; you never know."

I stumble into the street

And away from the keep.

A large oak looms ahead,

The moonlight shattering its windswept leaves.

A shriek splits the evening air.

Spinning, I whip the wire around his neck,

And slowly,

Strangle

A wretched little hermit

To death.

I rationalize:

"He must've been, well... mad, or stupid.

Either way, he was evil."

I climb another step on the stairway,

And fly the Great Winged one

Across the Milky Way,

And say a silent prayer,

"Leda, let's have fun tonight.

Leda, let's Wang Chung tonight."

Perchance to sleep. Perchance to dream.

As only Augmen dream.

 

Zippo

Circa 1990
