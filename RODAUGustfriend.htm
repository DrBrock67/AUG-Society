An AUGust Friend

I once found myself penniless an cold.

I had but thrice darned socks for my soiled feet.

A dashing chap confronted me and told

of my good fortune that we'd chanced to meet.

He offered me a room and daily meals

if I'd assist with tasks he had in mind.

We sat before his fire discussing deals,

enjoying drink, I know not of what kind.

When next I woke, my head and nethers throbbed,

unable to recall the nights events.

I wondered if perchance I had been robbed;

instead I was adorned in clothes he'd lent.

When fortune greets you as a wholesome friend,

Be confident you'll get it in the end.
