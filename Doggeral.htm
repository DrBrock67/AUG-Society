SONG OF THE OPEN BOATS (or, Let ‘em Eat Doggerel)  -- anonymous

 

Up on the White River

In the year of ‘85

Came a band of desperadoes

Who were trying to survive.

 

They camped beside the Norfork

With the great dam looming near

And gathered nightly round the shoplight

For good food and liquid cheer.

 

There was hardy Jim the photog,

Soon to risk both life and limb,

And Troy the ill-starred driver

Who kept vigil on the gin;

 

There was Rock, a man of secrets,

Slipping off to use the phone,

And Woodsy Randy like a coyote

Wandering restless before dawn.

 

With a fifth soul in their number

That they called The Other Guy,

They courted danger on the river

With their spirits soaring high.

 

Plowing upstream was a crapshoot

When chill fog engulfed the scene,

And the lads were kind of nervous

‘Til the trout cops judged them clean.

 

The crew met many a wonder,

Like frozen snakes and midday moons,

And manly voices filling twilight

With “rock-bock-a-boom-bomb, a-rock-bam-boom!”

 

The final fling became a legend--

Beef and booze, banana split.

Then the dawn brought grim reality:

The trip’s last line was writ.      

 

But one day, as sure as summer,

Back to the river they will throng,

Drawn by the glow of the shoplight

And the air compressor’s song.
