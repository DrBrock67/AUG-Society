TIP

 

The Game of AUG

 

TIP is a basketball game of shooting and rebounding prowess.  It combines forms of “Horse” and “21” in a golf-style scoring format.  Its origins are mystical, being brought into the fold by Ferret from a heretofore indeterminate source.  It is dominant.  It is not fuck-all. 

 

Objective

 

  • Become the last player standing with less than 10 points (see exception at Rule 5f below)

 

Players

 

  • 2 – 10

 

Equipment

 

  • The Ball – any size basketball will do

(use of WNBA ball requires host to wear shorts around ankles for duration of game)

  • The Goal – regulation is acceptable, but shorter goals are cooler

(by rule, players over 6’ in height must be hobbled prior to game commencement)

  • Court Dimensions – no limits; irregularities are cooler
  • The Surface – no limits; dirt is fuck-all
  • Player uniforms – absolutely not; players in “boat shoes” are revered

 

The Play

 

  1. A rotation is established that remains in effect for the duration of the game.  Rotation may be established in any of the following ways:

 

    1. Shoot for order (from approximate free-throw range, players shoot, with made shots determining rotation {1st made = 1st, 2nd made = 2nd, etc…})

 

    1. Cheez-all to Dominator (players shoot in order according to their finish in a previous game from the first out to the winner)

 

    1. Alphabetical by last name

 

    1. Youngest to oldest

 

    1. Most impatient to most apathetic

 

 

  1. The game commences with the first player in the rotation (hereafter referred to as the Shooter) taking a shot at the goal from any place he so chooses. 

 

    1. As there is no “court” per se, no boundaries exist to restrict the player.  The shot may be behind or in front of the goal, and may be attempted from any distance. 

 

    1. The point at which the Shooter’s feet last touched the ground (or, if the shooter is flat of foot when the shot is launched, the point where his feet exist) when the ball is launched is referred to as the “make spot”.

 

    1. If the shot fails to touch the rim, the Shooter is “awarded” with 1 point for an “air ball”.

 

    1. Note that, unlike in “Horse”, the type of shot is ignored, only the make spot is of import.  Bitch-ass “Kobe Bryant-behind-the-back-between-the-legs-in-your-mamma’s-kitchen-off-the-glass-and-in” shots are irrelevant.  The make spot is the make spot.  This will be of critical import once you start playing the game.

 

    1. Under rare circumstances of goal-tending, the offending party is “awarded” 2 points.  If the offending party is the would-be Obligato (described below), he also forfeits his turn.  Play proceeds with the next player in rotation.

 

  1. Once the Shooter launches a shot from the make spot, one of two things may happen:

 

    1. If the shot is made (having duly gone through the rim), the next player in rotation (hereafter referred to as the Obligato) must attempt a shot from the same make spot.  Here, one of two things may happen:

 

      1. If the Obligato makes his shot, he forfeits his turn.  The next player in rotation now becomes the Obligato and must attempt the shot from the same make spot. 

 

      1. If the shot misses, the Obligato is “awarded” 2 points. 

 

        • The Obligato is bound by the same rules as the Shooter; an air ball will result in an “award” of 1 additional point. 

 

        • Tippage ensues as described below (see Rule 4).

 

      1. Note that, if all Obligato shots are made (i.e., all players make the shot made by the original Shooter), the Shooter is “awarded” 1 point.  This is referred to as the “Obligato Domino”.

 

    1. If the shot misses, Tippage ensues as described below (see Rule 4).

 

  1. In the event of a missed shot by either the Shooter or an Obligato, the second phase of the game comes into play (commonly referred to as “Tippage”).  The next player in rotation following the Shooter or Obligato (hereafter referred to as the “Tipper”) may attempt to “tip” the ball in the basket by retrieving the ball  (while airborne) before it has touched the ground, any inanimate object other than the rim and backboard, or another player, and shooting the ball (while airborne) into the basket.  Such an accomplishment is heretofore referred to as a “legal tip”.

 

    1. If the Tipper succeeds under the rules (having been airborne upon receipt of ball and delivery of ball toward basket) in a made shot, the person who previously shot or tipped the ball is “awarded” with 1 point.

 

    1. If some player other than the player next in rotation inadvertently touches the ball following an errant shot by the Tipper, the ball is dead.  The next player in rotation (after the Shooter or last Obligato) now becomes the Shooter.

 

    1. If some player other than the player next in rotation deliberately obstructs, obfuscates, or hinders the Tipper in his opportunity for Tippage, the ball is dead.  The next player in rotation (after the Shooter or last Obligato) now becomes the Shooter.  The offender is the object of ridicule and scorn; repeat offenses warrant a “bag-kick” to be administered by the offended party.

 

    1. If the Tipper fails to make the shot, one of two things may happen:

 

      1. Assuming that the shot was a legal tip, the next player in rotation may attempt to “tip” the Tipper following the same guidelines as before.  Tippage may occur ad infinitum until the ball is dead under the rules heretofore described.

 

      1. The ball becomes dead because of an illegal tip or because the succeeding Tipper chooses not to attempt further Tippage.  The next player in rotation after the Shooter or last Obligato now becomes the Shooter.

 

(Note: Rule 2c – “air balls” do not apply to tips)

 

    1. If the Tipper chooses not to attempt Tippage (e.g., the difficulty level of the tip is not worth the risk of being tipped in turn), the ball is dead.  Such is his right.  Excessive reluctance on the part of a Tipper to attempt Tippage is not a violation per se, but may subject the player to ridicule and scorn.  The next player in rotation after the Shooter or last Obligato now becomes the Shooter.

 

  1. As points are “awarded” to players, each player keeps track of his point tally (also akin to golf in this regard). 

 

    1. It is common practice for all players to “sound off” with their point counts periodically throughout the game.  Errors in scoring are dealt with among the players in a manner befitting the transgression.  Itinerant cheaters do not play TIP.

 

    1. By way of summary, scores are “awarded” to players as follows:

 

      1. Obligato miss of a made shot – 2 points
      2. Obligato Domino on Shooter – 1 point
      3. “Air ball” by Shooter or Obligato – 1 point
      4. Tipped in by succeeding player – 1 point
      5. Goal-tending by any player – 2 points

 

    1. Any player whose point count reaches 10 or more is immediately out of the game.  He ceases to be a part of the rotation and may not participate in Tippage.  To continue to participate is a deliberate obstruction as in Rule 4c above.  There is but one exception to this rule (see Rule 5f).

 

    1. The last player with a score of less than 10 wins and is declared the “Dominator”.

 

    1. Some additional terms of the game are instructive though they do not directly impact game scoring:

 

      1. Slam dunks (or “slammage”) as a form of a tip do not afford extra credit per se.  They do however allow for ridicule and scorn to be heaped upon the recipient.

 

      1. “Utter Domination” is the term referring to the very rare instance where the winning player has a point count of 0.  At Cinco de AUG-o, the first instances of this heretofore legendary feat were documented, first by Rewd against Flathead and Wango, and then a short time later by Flathead against Rewd and Wango.

 

      1. In certain situations, a player may exit the game with a point count exceeding 10 (e.g., player has 9 points and misses a shot as an Obligato thereby attaining 11 points).  Two very rare situations can occur which, while not directly impacting the game, do afford a vast amount of ridicule and scorn to be heaped upon oneself.  These are:

 

        • The “douche” – a score of 12 points attained by a player with 9 points either “air balling” an Obligato shot (called “self-douching”), or having an Obligato shot tipped in (called “being douched”).  The latter is a play of particular bravado on the part of the “doucher” as no advantage is gained by tipping a shot in on a player already out of the game.
        • The exceedingly rare “double-douche” – a score of 13 points is only attainable if a player with 9 points “air balls” an Obligato shot which is then legally tipped in.  So rare is the “double douche” that its occurrence has only been documented in 2 instances throughout the history of time.  On one occasion such a fate befell Wango at Rewd’s house in the mid-1980’s.  With 9 points, Wango was forced to make an Obligato baseline jumper from behind a shrubbery; he overshot the goal and was forcibly and somewhat rudely the recipient of slammage.  The other occurrence fell upon Rewd at AUGFest II in AusTex.  Having utterly misgauged the recent removal of tree branches from around the goal, his shot fell short and was tipped in – achieving a self-fulfilling prophecy in which, earlier in the day, he had made the bold statement that he felt a “double-douche” would never occur again.

 

    1. A situation exists that is so exceedingly rare as to be almost unfathomable.  Yet it is an exception to the rule of “10 points and out” put in place only as a potential guard against “douche” attempts.  In the event that only 2 players remain and both players have 9 points, a player who misses an Obligato shot has one last chance to avoid losing the game.  If the Shooter attempts and misses a legal tip of the Obligato’s missed shot (ostensibly to “douche” his opponent on the final play of the game thereby attaining legendary status), the Obligato may attempt to tip the shooter in.  If the Obligato succeeds, both players return to 9 points (regardless of an “air ball”) and the ball goes to the Shooter.  Note that this exception applies throughout the duration of Tippage for that play.  This play is known only as “The Queef”.

 

  1. Frequently Asked Questions

 

    1. Can two players be eliminated on the same play?

 

Yes.  Such a situation may occur when an Obligato’s missed shot results in his elimination and a succeeding player with 9 points is also tipped in.  This typically occurs in the course of attempts at “douching” and is subject to the exception mentioned at Rule 5f.

 

    1. What does “Red Stick range” refer to?

 

Often thought to refer to Rewd’s former abode in Baton Rouge, it is actually a reference to a broken off bottle rocket stick that lay on the downward-sloping hill of the drainage ditch at Rewd’s residence in Paris.  The shot, of exceedingly difficult difficulty, was first attempted and made by Ferret.  A distance of some 25 feet away and roughly 4 feet below ground level at the goal, the shot was made with a “baseball” style launch.

 

    1. Can accommodations be made for less fortunately skilled players?

 

Yes.  The two most common accommodations are: 1) to forego the requirement of said disadvantaged player to be airborne during a tip attempt, and 2) to advance the make spot by 1 pace when the disadvantaged player is an Obligato, and retreat the make spot by 1 pace for all Obligato players when the disadvantaged player is a Shooter of a made spot.

 

    1. What is the “Ferret Gambit”?

 

This refers to a misinterpretation of Rule 2 (particularly, 2d) wherein Ferret accomplished a difficult shot from behind the goal and two feet to the side.  While Ferret’s made shot was accomplished by shooting over the backboard, Rewd rightfully made the Obligato shot by leaping from the make spot and performing slammage (the goal was of 7 foot height).  All other AUGers followed suit resulting in an Obligato Domino for Ferret, much to his chagrin.

 

    1. What is a “credit card” tip?

 

Due to the often lackluster leaping ability of many formidable TIP players, legal tips often occur while the Tipper is mere millimeters above the ground (roughly the thickness of a credit card – hence the name).  It is for this reason that vigilant observation by co-players is warranted.  If the would-be Tipper is not off the ground at time of tip, the ball is dead.
